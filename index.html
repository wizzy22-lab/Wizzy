01

Operator – HVAC Decision Support UX

Designed a system to help small business owners make better energy decisions

Design Challenge

Small business owners lack visibility into energy usage patterns, making cost-saving decisions feel arbitrary and overwhelming.

My Approach

Mapped operator decision flows, identified key friction points, and designed a dashboard that surfaces actionable insights over raw data.

Key Decisions

Prioritized decision-support framing over data visualization — every screen answers "what should I do next?" rather than "what happened?"

Outcome

A streamlined HVAC control UX that reduces cognitive load and helps operators make confident energy decisions independently.

02

BingX AI Master Redesign

Improved usability and trust in AI-driven trading UX

Design Challenge

AI trading features were powerful but opaque — users didn't trust outputs they couldn't understand or verify.

My Approach

Conducted a UX audit of the existing AI Master flow, identified trust gaps, and redesigned key screens to expose reasoning and reduce ambiguity.

Key Decisions

Added explainability layers and progressive disclosure — showing the "why" behind AI signals without overwhelming the core interface.

Outcome

Redesigned flow that improves perceived reliability of AI recommendations and reduces user hesitation at key decision points.

03

GEME – Sports Guest Matching Platform

Co-founded and designed a mobile platform for sports-based matching

Design Challenge

Sports players struggle to find suitable partners or fill open spots — existing solutions are fragmented across group chats and community boards.

My Approach

Designed end-to-end from problem definition through launch — including user research, information architecture, and full UI design.

Key Decisions

Built matching logic around skill level and availability rather than social graphs — reducing friction for users who want to play, not network.

Outcome

A launched mobile platform that enables sports-based guest matching with a clean, activity-first UX.

Weekend Greenwich – Premium Dessert Brand

Led brand experience and pop-up UX in retail environments

Design Challenge

My Approach

Challenge #1

Designing UX without a team or prior industry experience

Transitioning into UX with no formal team or mentorship, I had to self-direct my learning across research methods, information architecture, and interaction design. I built a habit of learning by doing — running my own usability tests, iterating on wireframes, and studying real products to close the gap between theory and practice.

Challenge #2

Translating operational intuition into design decisions

Years of running F&B operations gave me sharp instincts about what users actually need — but translating that into UX rationale required a new language. I learned to frame operational insight as user pain points, map decision flows from real-world processes, and articulate design choices in terms of user behavior rather than business habit.

Challenge #3

Building a product from concept to launch solo

With GEME, I handled every stage alone — problem definition, competitive research, user flows, UI design, and launch coordination. The constraint forced me to prioritize ruthlessly, make faster decisions with incomplete information, and maintain design coherence across the entire product without a handoff process.
